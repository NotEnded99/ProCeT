We propose a progressive depth phased repair algorithm for neural network-based control barrier functions (CBFs) that systematically corrects verification failures through a two-phase strategy. In the first phase, definitive violations (where the CBF condition $h(x) > 0$ in unsafe regions or $\nabla h(x) \cdot f(x) + \alpha(h(x)) < 0$ in safe regions) are repaired at low verification depth. In the second phase, uncertain regions (depth limit reached or cannot split) are progressively addressed as verification depth increases. Our method employs a randomized smoothing Jacobian estimator to compute gradient information without backpropagating through non-differentiable activation functions, combined with a quadratic programming projection that guarantees updated parameters preserve previously verified safe regions. Experiments on multiple dynamical systems demonstrate that our approach achieves significantly higher certified percentages compared to prior repair methods, with the progressive depth strategy enabling repair of violations that are undetectable at shallow depths but become deterministically verifiable at deeper levels.
